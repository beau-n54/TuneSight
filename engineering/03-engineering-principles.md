**Purpose:** Defines the engineering practices that apply the Engineering Constitution throughout the design, development and evolution of TuneSight.

**Status:** Ratified



# TuneSight

## Engineering Principles

---

## Introduction

The Engineering Constitution defines the immutable engineering laws of TuneSight.

The Engineering Principles define the engineering practices required to apply those laws consistently.

Every engineering decision, architectural design and implementation should remain faithful to these Principles.

---

## Principle 1

### Design for Engineering Understanding

Every engineering decision shall improve the platform's ability to produce accurate, explainable engineering understanding.

Designs that increase complexity without improving engineering understanding should be reconsidered before implementation.

---

## Principle 2

### Evidence Before Conclusion

Engineering conclusions shall only be formed after evaluating the available evidence.

Where evidence is incomplete, uncertainty shall be communicated.

Where evidence is contradictory, investigation shall continue until the contradiction is resolved or explicitly documented.

Confidence shall always reflect the quality of the available evidence.

---

## Principle 3

### Single Responsibility

Every engineering subsystem shall have one clearly defined responsibility.

Subsystem boundaries shall be explicitly defined before implementation.

Where responsibilities overlap, subsystem boundaries shall be redesigned before implementation proceeds.

No subsystem shall assume responsibilities that belong to another subsystem.

---

## Principle 4

### Canonical Knowledge

Engineering knowledge shall exist within one authoritative source.

Consumers shall reference the canonical source rather than maintaining independent copies.

Duplicate engineering knowledge shall be eliminated whenever it is identified.

---

## Principle 5

### Explainable Recommendations

Every engineering recommendation shall identify the evidence and reasoning that produced it.

Where engineering confidence forms part of a recommendation, that confidence shall be communicated honestly and remain consistent with the available evidence.

No recommendation shall rely upon unexplained reasoning.

---

## Principle 6

### Separation of Engineering and Presentation

Engineering logic shall remain independent of presentation.

Presentation layers shall consume engineering truth.

Presentation layers shall never determine, modify or create engineering truth.

---

## Principle 7

### Preserve Engineering Memory

Engineering knowledge that may improve future engineering reasoning shall be preserved within the platform's engineering memory.

Engineering understanding gained once should not require rediscovery unless new evidence invalidates previous conclusions.

---

## Principle 8

### Design for Evolution

Engineering decisions shall favour designs that accommodate future evolution without requiring fundamental architectural redesign.

Short-term implementation convenience shall never compromise long-term engineering integrity.

---

## Principle 9

### Deliberate Simplicity

Engineering solutions shall remain as simple as possible while accurately representing engineering reality.

Complexity shall only be introduced where it provides measurable engineering value.

Unnecessary complexity shall be removed whenever it is identified.

---

## Principle 10

### Protect Engineering Trust

Every engineering decision shall strengthen trust in the platform.

Engineering trust is strengthened through accuracy, honesty, consistency, explainability and evidence.

Engineering trust shall never depend upon unsupported certainty or misleading presentation.

---

## Closing Statement

These Engineering Principles define how the Engineering Constitution is applied throughout TuneSight.

Consistent application of these Principles preserves architectural integrity, strengthens engineering understanding and ensures every implementation remains faithful to the Founder's Vision.

---

## Responsibility

Every engineer entrusted with TuneSight shares the responsibility of applying these Principles consistently throughout the continued evolution of the platform.
